# IEE Community Autoclaves

## Overview

The Institute of Ecology and Evolution (IEE) maintains shared autoclaves for community research use in:

- Pacific Hall 317
- Pacific Hall 17A

These autoclaves are intended for use by trained members of the IEE research community. This page provides general expectations and guidance for safe and respectful shared use.

---

# General Expectations

Users are expected to:

- Follow all applicable University of Oregon and EH&S safety guidelines
- Use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Clean up after use
- Report problems promptly
- Be considerate of shared access and scheduling needs

Please do not leave materials unattended for extended periods after cycles complete.

---

# Training

Users should receive basic autoclave training from their lab, supervisor, or an experienced user before operating either machine independently. Training is conducted by Stu Johnson (stuartj@uoregon.edu), Mark Currey (mcurrey@uoregon.edu) or approved trainer.

If you are unsure how to operate a machine, load materials properly, or select the appropriate cycle, please ask for assistance before use.

---

# Safety

Autoclaves operate at high temperature and pressure and can cause serious burns or injury if used improperly.

Minimum recommended PPE includes:

- Closed-toe shoes
- Eye protection
- Heat-resistant gloves when unloading hot materials
- Appropriate lab attire

Users should:

- Allow pressure to fully release before opening the door
- Open doors cautiously to avoid steam exposure
- Use caution when handling liquids and recently sterilized materials
- Never autoclave sealed containers

---

# Approved Uses

Typical approved uses include:

- Sterilizing media
- Glassware
- Autoclave-safe plastics
- Biological waste following institutional guidelines

---

# Prohibited Materials

Do not autoclave:

- Volatile or flammable chemicals
- Solvents
- Bleach-containing materials
- Radioactive materials
- Sealed containers
- Materials not rated as autoclave safe

When in doubt, ask before use.

---

# Shared Use Guidelines

To help maintain fair community access:

- Avoid monopolizing machine time during peak hours
- Coordinate unusually large or long runs when possible
- Remove materials promptly after cycles finish
- Leave the autoclave area clean and ready for the next user

Please report spills or messes immediately and clean the area after use.

# Troubleshooting (Pacific 317)

## Common Issues

### Door Will Not Open

Possible causes:

- Chamber still pressurized
- Cycle not fully completed
- Safety interlock engaged

Actions:

- Wait several minutes and verify chamber pressure is zero
- Confirm cycle has ended completely
- Do not force the door open
- Contact the machine administrator if issue persists

---

### Wet Loads After Cycle

Possible causes:

- Overloaded chamber
- Improper bag closure
- Insufficient drying time

Actions:

- Reduce load size
- Loosen bag closures slightly
- Run additional dry time if appropriate

---

### Error Messages or Alarms

Actions:

- Record the exact error message/code
- Note the cycle type being used
- Report the issue to the administrator
- Do not repeatedly restart failed cycles without guidance

---

### Steam or Water Leaks

Actions:

- Stop using the machine
- Report the issue immediately
- Place signage on the machine if necessary to prevent further use
