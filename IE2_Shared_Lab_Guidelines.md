# Institute of Ecology and Evolution — Shared Laboratory Spaces

**User Guidelines | Version 1.1 | March 2026**

---

## 1. Purpose

The Shared Laboratories provide IE² research groups with access to molecular biology equipment and workspace on a temporary or shared basis. These guidelines are intended to support safe, equitable, and orderly use of the space.

The laboratory spaces are coordinated by the Research Coordinator, who serves as the primary point of contact for routine questions, access requests, scheduling, and day-to-day operations. The IE² Director serves as the alternate contact. Final decisions regarding access, storage allocation, equipment exceptions, and policy enforcement rest with IE² leadership.

## 2. Getting Access

To request access, send a brief email to the Research Coordinator and the IE² Director. There are no forms to complete. Please include:

- Your PI and research group
- A brief description of the work to be conducted
- Which space and what equipment you plan to use
- Roughly how often or for how long you'll need access
- Any anticipated storage needs

Currently two Shared Laboratory spaces are maintained by IE²:

| Space | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Onyx 368** | A "dirty" lab suitable for sample sorting, sample prep, and light molecular work (e.g., DNA extraction) |
| **Pacific 327** | A fully equipped molecular biology lab |

Both spaces are only suitable for **BSL1** work (see Section 5).

Access is not active until the request has been reviewed and the required orientation has been completed. If you wish to use equipment with which you are unfamiliar, the IE² Research Coordinator will provide training (see Section 4). Orientation covers general access and safety procedures; additional equipment-specific training may be required before independent use of particular instruments. IE² may limit, suspend, or revoke access when necessary because of safety concerns, misuse, space constraints, or repeated failure to follow these guidelines.

### Scheduling

Bench time and major equipment should be booked in advance through the Research Coordinator. Please include the requested date and the expected duration of use. Users are expected to keep bookings reasonable, release time they no longer need, and avoid reserving equipment speculatively. Repeated no-shows or chronic overbooking may result in reduced scheduling priority or review of access.

---

## 3. Lab Conduct and Housekeeping

Please leave the lab in the same condition in which you would want to find it. At a minimum:

- Wipe down benches, return equipment to where you found it, and take your personal items with you.
- Do not leave reagents, samples, or procedures unattended overnight unless this has been cleared in advance and the materials are clearly labeled.
- Clean up spills immediately and report any spill, exposure, or unsafe condition that requires follow-up.
- Keep shared sinks, waste stations, and benches clear, and label all materials left in shared areas.
- Users are responsible for taking their own cardboard waste to the dumpster.

---

## 4. Equipment and Shared Resources

### Training and Responsibility

Use of shared equipment is limited to users who have completed any required orientation or training designated by the IE² Research Coordinator or other authorized personnel. Training may include direct instruction and, where appropriate, sign-off before independent use. Routine upkeep and service of shared equipment will be coordinated through IE², with cost allocation determined by IE² leadership and relevant faculty as appropriate to the equipment and its use. If equipment is damaged through misuse, unauthorized use, or failure to follow posted procedures or training instructions, IE² leadership may assign responsibility for repair or replacement costs to the responsible lab or user. Routine maintenance costs will be covered by IE².

### General Equipment Rules

- Equipment stays in the lab unless removal has been approved in writing by the Research Coordinator and the IE² Director.
- Major instruments (centrifuges, thermocyclers, gel imaging systems) should only be used by trained users. For use of unfamiliar equipment, contact the Research Coordinator for training and instructions.
- Any malfunction, damage, or unsafe operation must be reported promptly to the Research Coordinator.
- Users should not attempt repairs, arrange service, or modify equipment settings outside normal operation unless specifically authorized to do so.

Requests to use equipment off-site or outside the ordinary scope of approved work must be submitted in writing and approved in advance.

---

## 5. Hazardous Materials and Chemicals

- All work in the Shared Molecular Laboratory must comply with applicable University of Oregon Environmental Health and Safety (EHS), biosafety, chemical hygiene, and waste-disposal requirements.
- Hazardous materials — including flammables, carcinogens, mutagens, biological agents, and compressed gases — may not be brought into the laboratory without prior approval from the Research Coordinator.
- Approved substances must be properly labeled, appropriately stored, and accompanied by accessible Safety Data Sheets.
- Do not use legacy, unlabeled, or uncertain chemicals or biological materials found in the laboratory without confirmation that they are identified, approved, and appropriate for your work.
- Chemical and biological waste must be disposed of in accordance with UO EHS requirements.
- Spills, exposures, injuries, or other safety incidents must be reported promptly to the Research Coordinator and, when appropriate, through established university safety channels.
- **All spaces are considered BSL1** — work that requires BSL2 or above facilities cannot be done in IE² Shared Laboratories.

---

## 6. Cold Storage

Freezer and refrigerator space is limited and allocated on a first-approved basis. To request dedicated storage, email the Research Coordinator and the IE² Director with:

- What you plan to store
- Volume and temperature requirements
- How long you expect to need the space

All stored materials must be labeled clearly with **user name, research group, date, and contents**. Unlabeled, expired, or abandoned items may be removed during periodic cleanouts, with notice where feasible.

Short-term storage during an active session is permitted without prior approval, provided that materials are removed when the session is complete. Do not access another group's storage without permission.

---

## 7. Consumables

Users are expected to bring all materials needed for their own work. Do not assume that any reagents, plastics, or other supplies present in the laboratory are communal. Use of another group's consumables requires explicit permission. If any questions arise they can be directed to the Research Coordinator.

There may be a limited supply of shared stock with a sign-out and replacement system for certain items, but do not assume this before starting work.

---

## 8. Violations and Access Review

Failure to follow these guidelines may result in a warning, temporary restriction of access, loss of scheduling privileges, removal of stored materials, or suspension of laboratory use, depending on the nature and severity of the issue. Serious safety violations may result in immediate restriction of access pending review by IE² leadership. Any conflicts that arise should be immediately brought to the attention of the IE² Director and the Research Coordinator.

---

## 9. Amendments and Review

These guidelines will be reviewed annually (or sooner if needed). Users are responsible for complying with the most current version distributed by IE².
